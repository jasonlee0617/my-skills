# Decision Audit

Examine a decision without pretending certainty. Separate confirmed facts, inferences, unknowns, and recommendations, then prioritize the findings that could materially change the outcome.

## Audit Workflow

1. Restate the decision, objective, time horizon, and known constraints in a few lines.
2. Inspect available files, evidence, and prior decisions before declaring information missing.
3. Apply the five audit lenses below.
4. Rank findings by impact and urgency; do not give every observation equal weight.
5. End with the smallest set of next actions that would reduce the most uncertainty or risk.

## Five Audit Lenses

### 1. Information Blind Spots

Answer: "What am I least certain about right now?"

- List six or seven materially under-investigated items when the available context supports that many; do not pad the list with trivialities.
- For each item, explain why the gap exists, why it is difficult to understand, and what evidence would resolve it.
- Do not present a firm conclusion when a critical information gap remains.

### 2. Unnoticed Cognitive Gaps

Answer: "What is the biggest omission in my current understanding, including information I may not realize I need?"

- Surface hidden assumptions, missing stakeholders, second-order effects, base-rate neglect, and framing errors.
- Distinguish an unknown fact from an unknown category of facts.

### 3. Failure Pre-Mortem

Assume the proposal has failed three months from now.

- Identify the most plausible causes of failure.
- Connect each cause to an early warning signal and a practical mitigation.
- Prioritize realistic failure modes over dramatic but unlikely scenarios.

### 4. Leading-Edge Opportunity

Answer: "If one industry-leading capability could be added, what should it be?"

- Recommend one capability with a clear user or operational advantage.
- Explain why it is a better choice than nearby alternatives.
- State its cost, dependency, and risk so brainstorming does not become an unbounded feature list.

### 5. Communication Improvement

Answer: "What different approach would make this conversation more efficient and decision-ready?"

- Identify unclear questions, repeated context, missing evidence, or decisions without owners.
- Recommend a more efficient question order, artifact, or decision format.
- Do not blame the user for information that could have been discovered from the workspace.

## Output Format

Use this structure unless the user requests another format:

1. Decision and current evidence
2. Highest-impact blind spots
3. Largest unnoticed omission
4. Three-month failure pre-mortem
5. One leading-edge opportunity
6. Communication improvements
7. Prioritized next actions

Label uncertain claims explicitly. Keep recommendations proportional to the available evidence and avoid inventing facts to fill gaps.
